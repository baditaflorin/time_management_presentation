Time Management refers to a set of principles, practices, skills, and systems used to control the time spent on specific activities, with the aim of increasing work efficiency and productivity.

Choice Over Duty

  • List on a piece of paper all those things you tell yourself you have to do.
  • What activities do you do in your life that you don’t experience as playful?
  • List any activity you avoid but still do because you feel you have no choice.
  • Put the words "I choose..." in front of each item on the list.
  • When you’re done with your list, acknowledge that you do these things because you choose to, not because you have to.
  • Write for each item "I choose because I want to" in connection with the intention.

Problem Definition

  • WHAT? The problem? The impact of the problem? Measurable
  • WHEN? Or under what conditions does the problem occur? Time
  • WHERE? Where does the problem occur? Location
  • WHO? Who is interested? Who suffers the consequences? Who is concerned?
  • WHY? Is it worth doing this project? Ask "why?" 5 times (Deming)
  • HOW? How does the problem occur? How do we evaluate, compare, or differentiate?
