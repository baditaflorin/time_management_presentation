What Does Time Management Mean?

Time Management refers to a set of principles, practices, skills, and systems used to control the time spent on specific activities, with the aim of increasing work efficiency and productivity.

Avoiding Obligation

Activity carried out out of obligation, duty, fear, guilt, or shame will lose its joy and eventually lead to resistance.

Choice Over Duty

I CHOOSE TO ______ BECAUSE I WANT TO ____________

  • List on a piece of paper all those things you tell yourself you have to do.
  • What activities do you do in your life that you don’t experience as playful?
  • List any activity you avoid but still do because you feel you have no choice.
  • Put the words "I choose..." in front of each item on the list.
  • When you’re done with your list, acknowledge that you do these things because you choose to, not because you have to.
  • Write for each item "I choose because I want to" in connection with the intention.

Problem Definition

  • WHAT? The problem? The impact of the problem? Measurable
  • WHEN? Or under what conditions does the problem occur? Time
  • WHERE? Where does the problem occur? Location
  • WHO? Who is interested? Who suffers the consequences? Who is concerned?
  • WHY? Is it worth doing this project? Ask "why?" 5 times (Deming)
  • HOW? How does the problem occur? How do we evaluate, compare, or differentiate?

Problem Tree

The Tree:

  • Analysis of an existing situation
  • Identification of key problems in context
  • Visualization of problems in the form of a tree diagram (cause-effect relationship)

Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)

Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa):

  • Used to note ideas and theories needed to solve a problem in production or processes.
  • The fish head is the effect to be increased or reduced; large bones indicate main categories of potential causes, and smaller bones are minor categories.

CAUSES

Equipment, People, Probes

How to Create Cause-and-Effect Diagrams:

Also known as an Ishikawa or "fishbone" diagram, it is used to explore and display the possible causes of a certain effect.

It helps in understanding market growth or decline... thus positioning, potential, and direction for a business or organization.

The SWOT Analysis provides a good framework for reviewing an organization’s strategy, positioning an organization, or analyzing any idea...
